Work

Vanguard Soaps

I would like to say my technical career began right out of high school, when I had my first internship at Vanguard Soaps. I was an "Engineer Intern" in which I coded in JavaScript and SQL. I also did significant work aiding in the creation of manufacturing process guidelines for the machine operators. You can find Vanguard Soap's website here.

Roblox Corporation

During the pandemic, I worked with my friend Grant Fink and some other contributors on a couple Roblox games. I designed a new virtual combat weapon system, which utilizes physics extrapolation and secure client-side response-times to deliver the optimally performant experience. I also included a feature that verifies projectile-to-player collisions by using a clock and physics recording system. Without diving into the technical details, collisions are verified by examining a player's perceived position at a given time compared to what the server saw at that time. If the difference is within a certain amount, the "hit" is approved. Otherwise, the "hit" is denied. You can find one of the games I worked on here. I was paid by Roblox as a contractor.

I've continued to work on Roblox games and have received compensation. As of the past year or so, I've worked on Trade City. I've added a secure marketplace, virtual currencies, minigames such as Jackpot, Rock-Paper-Scissors, Tic-Tac-Toe, and other features.

UMRF Ventures

I was hired at UMRF Ventures as Systems Analyst in April of 2022. UMRF Ventures is a partner company to FedEx Services and the University of Memphis. My department (Systems Analytics) employs students whom work with real engineering and IT teams to onboard their applications into Splunk in order for them to make searchable, organized business intelligence out of their ugly machine data. In order to do so, we (the systems analysts) utilize Git, Python, RegEx, and many other languages and technologies. So far, I've likely onboarded hundreds of FedEx Services applications into Splunk. UMRF's website can be found here.

University of Memphis - Center for Information Assurance

For the summer of 2022, I decided to do research for my university, specifically for the Center for Information Assurance. I've researched cybersecurity in critical infrastructure, including doing a case study on the Hampton Roads Sanitation District's ransomware incident. I also have worked with Arduino computer kits a lot, and have programmed them (in C) to do neat things, such as fading light bulbs, changing bulbs colors, making motors work, and more. I have more interesting projects coming up as well. For these projects I am working under the revered professor Dr. Dipankar Dasgupta, who is a nationally recognized computer research scientist. The website for the Center for Information Assurance can be found here.

Roblox Projects

I started tinkering with scripts on the Roblox platform when I was 12. I then moved on to combining scripts together to create interesting games and systems, but I still did not code much myself. However, by the time I was 15, I was able to make a full-fledged game. I accomplished this in 2017, when I was 16 or 17. The game's purpose was to be an "arena", an environment in which people play with virtual swords.

I created some other games between those couple years using that same design. But eventually (in 2019), I decided I want to create an automated game (a game that runs without needing an admin to execute commands). And so I did! You can find that game here. It's a complete, round-based combat game with levels, points, leaderboards, and more.

Here's a YouTube video someone made on my game. It got 100K+ views!

I was particularly proud of this game. It largely uses a single-script architecture (one script for server, one for client). It had many features and was fun to play. It acquired almost half a million visits.

I've developed many other games for a gaming community within Roblox. Typically these are arena-style combat games that utilize a similar admin command structure to the one I described above.
